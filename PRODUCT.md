# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Working software engineers and the technically curious who land on a post about
software architecture, terminals, or low-level systems, often via a search
result or a shared link. Many read in Vietnamese as a first language; many read
in English. They arrive mid-context, on any device, usually to read one specific
thing, and they decide within seconds whether this is a place worth their
attention and worth returning to.

## Product Purpose

`memo` is Hiep Lam's personal blog and notebook: long-form deep dives and
explainers, frequently published in both English and Vietnamese. It exists to
think in public, to make hard systems topics legible, and to be a durable
reference the author and readers come back to. Success is a reader who finishes
one piece and chooses to read a second, then remembers where they read it.

## Brand Personality

Chill, comfortable, unhurried. The voice of a good notebook read in a quiet
study, not a lecture and not a launch. Calm and confident: it knows the material
well enough to explain it plainly, without theatrics. Three words: **warm,
considered, unhurried.**

## Anti-references

- **No "hacker / matrix / terminal" costume.** No neon-green-on-black, no
  glitch, no fake-CRT, no "developer aesthetic" as decoration.
- **Not colorful.** No busy multi-hue palettes; color is used sparingly and
  warmly, never as confetti.
- **Not high-contrast or harsh.** No stark pure-white-on-pure-black, no bright /
  saturated / glaring surfaces. Easy on the eyes for long reading sessions.
- **Not a content-marketing blog.** No hero-metric template, no SaaS landing
  tropes, no clickbait furniture.

## Design Principles

1. **The reading is the product.** Every decision serves comfortable, sustained
   long-form reading. Chrome recedes; prose leads.
2. **Warm restraint, not cold minimalism.** Calm and quiet, but with the warmth
   of worn paper and soft daylight, never sterile or gray.
3. **Path to the second post.** The site actively, gently helps a reader who
   finished one piece find the next worthwhile one.
4. **Bilingual by default, never an afterthought.** EN/VI parity is a
   first-class part of the experience, not a bolt-on toggle.
5. **Show your work, plainly.** Confidence through clear explanation, not jargon
   or spectacle. Respect the reader's intelligence and their time.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA as the floor: body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥ 3:1, even
  within the deliberately soft "low-contrast" Sage palette. "Easy on the eyes"
  is achieved with warm hues and reduced glare, **not** by dropping below legible
  contrast.
- Full keyboard and screen-reader support (already a project feature); preserve
  visible focus states through the re-theme.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for any added motion.
- Light and dark modes both first-class; neither is an afterthought.
- Bilingual EN/VI content with correct `lang` attributes.
